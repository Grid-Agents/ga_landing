# Grid Agents — brand context

Extracted from the Energy Innovation Basecamp 2026 pitch deck (`~/Downloads/Basecamp presentation.pdf`, 26 slides). This file is the canonical brand brief for the landing page. Planner and critic both read this; the executor reads it via the planner's plan.

## What Grid Agents is

A digital workforce for the Distribution Network Operator (DNO) sector. Grid Agents builds AI agents that automate the review and assessment of distribution-level grid connection applications — covering generation, storage, and demand projects from **50 kW up to transmission level**.

The wedge is **Grid Connection Assessments** — the highest-leverage intervention point in DNO operations. The deck frames this as the challenge at the heart of **EIP176 (Energy Innovation Basecamp 2026, Future Workforce)**, run by the Energy Networks Association (ENA).

## The problem (one paragraph)

The DNO sector doesn't have a headcount problem — it has a knowledge problem. Experienced engineers retire each year taking decades of judgement with them; new hires take years to reach independence; and grid connection application volumes grow far faster than the workforce can scale. Without intervention, the sector faces a compounding bottleneck: too few experienced engineers, too many connection applications, and no mechanism to transfer expertise before it's lost. Today, skilled engineers spend most of their time manually reviewing complex unstructured multi-document application packs — checking for missing documents, screening for compliance gaps, chasing developers for corrections — rather than the complex technical judgement they are trained for.

## The solution (one paragraph)

AI agents ingest the full document pack (single-line diagrams, power flow studies, planning consents, land rights — everything), then run automated review and analysis along four stages: **completeness → compliance → consistency → readiness**. Developers are contacted directly with plain-language feedback by email, message, or voice. The proprietary knowledge base is trained on historical connection decisions, encoding the judgement of senior engineers into a model that improves with every application it processes.

## Five USPs (in the deck's order)

1. **Effective knowledge capture and transfer** — connection assessment is not just rule-following; it's judgement. Years of tacit knowledge (which edge cases to flag, how to interpret ambiguous diagrams, where network constraints bite) live in people, not systems. Grid Agents encodes that judgement.
2. **Training & onboarding acceleration** — from day one, new engineers work alongside an AI that surfaces how similar applications have been assessed, what experienced colleagues flagged, and how issues were resolved.
3. **Capacity release** — estimated **70–95%** reduction in per-application engineer time; effectively 2–3× throughput without additional recruitment.
4. **Improved customer journeys** — developers currently experience months of silence and confusion. Faster, clearer feedback directly improves Ofgem's connection survey quality scores, turning a regulatory liability into a competitive differentiator.
5. **Faster connection reviews** — slow reviews aren't just inefficient; they're costly.

## Regulator / urgency proof point

National Grid Electricity Transmission (NGET) fell short on Timely Connections and Quality of Connection Surveys in 2024/25:

- **12** untimely offers issued
- **6.5** survey quality score (vs 7.7 target)
- **£7.46m** combined penalties in one year

Ofgem cited three root causes: application volumes exceeding queue capacity, projects modifying requests and causing cascading rework, and changing network conditions adding complexity to every review.

## Tone & language

- **Sober and institutional.** Long, precise sentences. No exclamation marks, no growth-hacker phrasing, no "supercharge", "unlock", "revolutionise". Read like a McKinsey memo or a Sequoia portfolio note.
- **Insider-credible.** Use the real vocabulary: DNO, ENA, EIP176, G99, Ofgem, single-line diagram, power flow study, planning consent. Don't define these — the audience knows them.
- **Quantified where possible.** "70–95% reduction in engineer time per application", "£7.46m combined penalties", not "much faster" or "huge savings".

## What the landing page is *not*

- Not a product walkthrough or feature catalogue.
- Not a pricing page.
- Not a blog or content marketing surface.
- Not a recruiting page.

It exists to convert two audiences into pilot conversations: **DNO decision-makers** (procurement, ops, innovation) and **investors**. The primary CTA is **Book a pilot / demo call** (contact address: `hello@grid-agents.com`).
